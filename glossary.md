# Glossary of Terms

Most terms related to computers or technical elements come from computer science and cybersecurity.

Most terms related to methods, ways of practice, mindset, and rules come from Japanese martial arts (`budō`).

## Martial Arts Terms used in Cyberjūtsu

- <a name="dojo">Dojo</a> 道場 (Practice space)
  - A place of practice, training, or disciplined study.
  - In martial arts, the dojo is both a physical training space and a symbolic place of learning, etiquette, and self-development.
  - In Cyberjūtsu, the dojo is typically an isolated digital training environment used through cyberdecks.
- <a name="cyberjutsuka">Cyberjutsuka</a> サイバー術家 (Practitioner)
  - A Cyberjūtsu practitioner
- <a name="sensei">Sensei</a> 先生 (instructor)
  - Teacher, instructor, or one who has gone before in experience and practice.
  - In budo, sensei refers to the person who guides learning, transmits principles, and maintains discipline in practice.
- <a name="waza">Waza</a> 技 (technique)
  - A technique, method, or skillful action used to achieve an intended effect.
  - In martial arts, waza refers to a teachable and repeatable technical action or sequence expressing the principles of the system.
  - Depending on the discipline, a waza may be offensive, defensive, controlling, transitional, solo, or paired.
  - In Cyberjūtsu, the term is specialized for command-line-based techniques used in observation, attack, defense, and control.
- <a name="uke">Uke</a> 受け (defender,receiver)
  - The person who receives, undergoes, or defends against the action of the partner.
  - Depending on the discipline, uke may be the receiver of a technique, the attacked person, or the defensive role in paired practice.
- <a name="tori">Tori</a> 取り (attacker,taker)
  - The person who takes, applies, or leads the technique.
  - Depending on the discipline, tori may be the attacking, controlling, or initiating role in paired practice.
- <a name="uke-waza">Uke Waza</a> 受け技 (defense technique)
  - A defensive or receiving technique.
  - In some systems, the term refers to techniques used to block, receive, absorb, redirect, or protect against an attack.
- <a name="kogeki-waza">Kōgeki Waza</a> 攻撃技 (attack technique)
  - An attacking or offensive technique.
  - In some systems, the term refers to techniques used to initiate action, strike, enter, pressure, or otherwise take offensive initiative.
- <a name="bogyo-waza">Bogyo Waza</a> 防御技 (defense technique)
  - A defensive or protective technique.
  - The term refers broadly to techniques whose purpose is protection, preservation, or defensive control.
- <a name="suki">Suki</a> 隙技 (weakness,opening)
  - Opening or a Weakness in one's technique or posture
- <a name="yomi-waza">Yomi Waza</a> 読み技 (monitoring technique)
  - Literally, a "reading technique."
  - In a broad sense, the term can describe techniques of perception, interpretation, anticipation, and understanding of the opponent, the situation, or the environment.
  - In Cyberjūtsu, the term is specialized for monitoring and reading technical activity.
- <a name="kumi-kata">Kumi Kata</a> 組む方 (to link, to put together, to grapple, to assemble)
  - The way of linking, assembling, or engaging with a partner.
  - In martial disciplines, the term is often used for methods of gripping, connecting, or establishing a structured relationship with the opponent.
  - In Cyberjūtsu, the term is specialized for the exchange created through connected cyberdecks.
- <a name="rei">Rei</a> 礼 (welcome,respect)
  - Respect, courtesy, or formal etiquette.
  - In budo, rei structures the respectful relationship to the dojo, the teacher, the partner, and the practice itself.
  - It is often expressed by bowing or by formal verbal acknowledgment.
- <a name="hadjime">Hadjime</a> はじめ (Beginning)
  - Beginning, start, or commence.
  - In training, it is the command to begin the exercise, exchange, or practice.
- <a name="matte">Matte</a> 待て (Stop,Break)
  - Wait, stop, or temporary break.
  - In training, it is the command to pause the action.
- <a name="sono-mama">Sono mama</a> 其のまま (freeze)
  - Freeze the situation. No action. Hold the position.
- <a name="sore-made">Sore made</a> 其れ迄に (End) 
  - That is all, end, or finish.
  - In training, it is the command that ends the activity or exchange.
- <a name="ippon">Ippon</a> 一本 (Knockout)
  - One full point, one decisive score, or one complete result.
  - In many Japanese martial arts and combat sports, ippon represents a decisive and complete success according to the rules of the discipline.
- <a name="oasaekomi">Osae Komi</a> 押さえ込み (Full control)
  - Holding down, pinning, or controlling the opponent.
  - In grappling contexts, osae komi refers to a controlling immobilization maintained with structure and pressure.
- <a name="kata">Kata</a> 形 (Forms)
  - A formalized pattern or form used to preserve and transmit the principles of a discipline.
  - A kata may be solo or paired depending on the martial system.
  - A kata is not only choreography: it embodies technical, tactical, rhythmic, and educational principles through repeatable structure.
- <a name="randori">Randori</a> 乱取り (free practice or sparring)
  - Free practice in which partners apply techniques with a degree of resistance, adaptation, and spontaneity. 
  *The term is used in aikido, judo, and Brazilian jiu-jitsu dojos outside Japan. In Japan, this form of practice is called taninzu-gake (多人数掛け), which literally means multiple attackers. In karate the word kumite is usually used.* [wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Randori)
- <a name="kakutou">Kakutō</a> 格闘 (combat)
  - Combat or close combat between opponents.
  - In Cyberjūtsu, the term is used to describe the direct technical confrontation between practitioners, not only physical striking or grappling.
- <a name="uchikomi">Uchikomi</a> 打ち込み (Repetition training) 
  - Repetitive entry practice used to refine a technique.
  - The emphasis is usually on precision, timing, positioning, and repetition rather than full completion.
- <a name="nagekomi">Nagekomi</a> 打ち込み (Repetition training) 
  - Repetitive practice of throwing or of full execution after entry and preparation.
  - Compared with uchikomi, nagekomi generally includes fuller completion of the technique.
- <a name="yakusoku-geiko">Yakusoku geiko</a> 約束稽古 (agreed-upon practice)
  - Agreed-upon practice performed with predefined roles, attacks, responses, or constraints.
  - It is used to train timing, structure, and application under controlled conditions.
- <a name="seiryoku-zen-yo">Seiryoku zen yo</a> 精力最活用 (Maximum efficiency)
  - <b>Maximum efficiency with minimum effort.</b>
  - The principle that the desired result should be achieved through the most appropriate and efficient use of energy, body, mind, timing, and technique.
- <a name="jita-koei">Jita Kyôei</a> 自他共栄 (Mutual aid)
  - Mutual welfare and benefit, or mutual prosperity for self and others.
  - The principle that progress should contribute to both oneself and the wider community of practice.
- <a name="tandoku-renshu">Tandoku Renshu</a> 単独練習 (solo practice)
  - Solo practice performed without a partner.
  - It is used to refine form, coordination, memory, and internalization through repeated individual work.
- <a name="tsune">Tsune</a> 常 (training habits)
  - <b>Continuous practice as a permanent ritual to make progress.</b> 
  - It expresses constancy, regularity, and the habit of sustained training over time.
- <a name="shuhari">Shuhari</a> 守破離 (apply,detach,transcend)
  - `shu` (守): protect, obey, or preserve the fundamentals and forms.
  - `ha` (破): break, detach, or move beyond strict imitation to understand variation and principle.
  - `ri` (離): transcend, internalize, and act naturally without clinging to form.
- <a name="shin-gi-tai">Shin Gi Tai</a> 心技体 (mind,technique,fitness)
  - The unity of mind, technique, and body.
  - `Shin` represents mind, spirit, and intention. `Gi` represents technique, skill, and knowledge. `Tai` represents body, physical condition, and embodied action.
  - The three must be trained together to reach balanced development and effective practice.
- <a name="tsukuri">Tsukuri</a> 作り (construct/prepare)
  - Preparation or construction of the conditions needed for successful execution.
  - In martial practice, tsukuri is the phase in which position, timing, structure, and opportunity are prepared.
- <a name="kusushi">Kuzushi</a> 崩し (destroy/orient)
  - Breaking balance, structure, posture, or stability.
  - Kuzushi is the act of creating a disadvantage in the opponent or in the situation so that the next action becomes possible.
- <a name="kake">Kake</a> 懸け (exploit/execute)
  - Execution or completion of the technique.
  - Kake is the phase in which the prepared opportunity is committed to and carried through.
- <a name="mikiri">Mikiri</a> 見切る (perception)
  - Perception, discernment, or cutting through appearances to understand the situation.
  - In martial practice, mikiri is the ability to read movement, weakness, timing, and intention.
- <a name="go-no-sen">Go No Sen</a> 後の先 (post-initiative)
  - Taking initiative after the opponent has already begun to act.
  - It is a form of responsive initiative in which one answers the opponent's action with correct timing.
- <a name="sen-no-sen">Sen No Sen</a> 先の先 (initiative)
  - Taking initiative at the very moment the opponent's action begins or is about to begin.
  - It is an intercepting initiative based on timing and perception.
- <a name="sen-sen-no-sen">Sen sen no sen</a> 先先の先 (superior initiative)
  - Superior or pre-emptive initiative taken before the opponent's action fully emerges.
  - It depends on reading intention, mentality, and opportunity with high precision.
- <a name="kaeshi">Kaeshi</a> 返 (counterattack return-favour)
  - Return, reversal, or counter.
  - In martial practice, kaeshi refers to reversing the situation by responding to the opponent's action with an effective counter.
- <a name="mokuso">Mokusō</a> 黙想 (meditation,mind training)
  - Silent reflection, meditation, or inward concentration.
  - In training, mokusō is used to calm the mind, focus attention, and integrate practice.

## Cyber terms
- <a name="cyberdeck">Cyberdeck</a> (practitioner material)
  - In Cyberjūtsu, a cyberdeck is the practitioner’s standard training device.
  - It is intended to provide the same hardware and software baseline for all practitioners so that progression depends on people rather than equipment differences.
  - A cyberdeck usually includes a screen, a computer, a keyboard, and a network connection. See [cyberdeck project](https://github.com/wocsa/cyberdeck)
- <a name="port">Port</a> (network connection number)
  - A port is a numbered network endpoint used by a service to send or receive communication.
- <a name="cli">Command Line Interface</a> (cli)
  - A command line interface is a text-based interface used to read information and enter commands.
- <a name="command">Command</a>
  - A command is an instruction entered by a user to request an action from a system.
- <a name="log">Log</a>
  - A log is a recorded trace of events, usually including time, context, and system information.
