# Jujutsu Kata: Reference Sources

Checked on 2026-05-17.

This note uses `jujutsu` for historical accuracy, though `jujitsu` is also common in English.

## Reliability note

I did not find a single official English source that publishes a complete canonical list of all traditional jujutsu kata across schools.

What can be documented reliably in English is:
- Kodokan's preserved classical and combative kata with clear official pages.
- Official classical school websites that confirm structured inherited curricula, even when they do not publish full kata lists in English.

## Best official sources

### 1. Kodokan Global: Koshiki-no-Kata

Source: <https://kdkjd.org/koshiki-no-kata/>

Why it matters:
- Official Kodokan English page.
- Best official English source for preserved classical `Kitō-ryū`-derived jujutsu forms.
- Publicly lists all 21 techniques.

Coverage:
- `Omote`: Tai, Yume-no-uchi, Ryoku-hi, Mizu-guruma, Mizu-nagare, Hiki-otoshi, Ko-daore, Uchi-kudaki, Tani-otoshi, Kuruma-daore, Shikoro-dori, Shikoro-gaeshi, Yu-dachi, Taki-otoshi
- `Ura`: Mi-kudaki, Kuruma-gaeshi, Mizu-iri, Ryu-setsu, Saka-otoshi, Yuki-ore, Iwa-nami

### 2. Kodokan Global: Kime-no-Kata

Source: <https://kdkjd.org/kime-no-kata/>

Why it matters:
- Official Kodokan English page.
- Strong official source for a formal self-defense / serious combat kata set rooted in older jujutsu combative logic.

Coverage:
- `Idori`: Ryote-dori, Tsukkake, Suri-age, Yoko-uchi, Ushiro-dori, Tsukkomi, Kiri-komi, Yoko-tsuki
- `Tachiai`: Ryote-dori, Sode-tori, Tsukkake, Tsuki-age, Suri-age, Yoko-uchi, Ke-age, Ushiro-dori, Tsukkomi, Kiri-komi, Nuki-gake, Kiri-oroshi

### 3. Kodokan Global: Kodokan Goshin-jutsu

Source: <https://kdkjd.org/1721-2/>

Why it matters:
- Official Kodokan English page.
- Formal paired self-defense kata with explicit unarmed and weapons sections.

Coverage:
- `Toshu-no-bu (unarmed)`: Ryote-dori, Hidari-eri-dori, Migi-eri-dori, Kata-ude-dori, Ushiro-eri-dori, Ushiro-jime, Kakae-dori, Naname-uchi, Ago-tsuki, Ganmem-tsuki, Mae-geri, Yoko-geri
- `Buki-no-bu (weapons)`: Tsukkake, Choku-tsuki, Naname-tsuki, Furi-age, Furi-oroshi, Morote-tsuki, Shomen-zuke, Koshi-gamae, Haimen-zuke

### 4. Kodokan Global: Kata overview

Source: <https://kdkjd.org/kata/>

Why it matters:
- Official Kodokan overview page.
- Best short institutional page to situate `Kime-no-Kata`, `Kodokan Goshin-jutsu`, and `Koshiki-no-Kata` together.

Most useful points:
- `Kime-no-Kata` is described as learning techniques for serious combative fight.
- `Kodokan Goshin-jutsu` is described as Kodokan self-defense forms.
- `Koshiki-no-Kata` is explicitly tied to `Kitō-ryū jujutsu`.

### 5. Takenouchi-ryu official site

Introduction: <https://www.takenouchi-ryu.org/english/introduction/>

Curriculum / initiation page: <https://www.takenouchi-ryu.org/english/keikoba-initiation/>

Why it matters:
- Official English website of a classical jujutsu school.
- Strong source for confirming that traditional jujutsu schools preserve broader kata-based curricula beyond Kodokan's published sets.

Most useful points from the site:
- Takenouchi-ryu presents itself as an old jujutsu tradition.
- It describes inherited sections such as `Koshinomawari`, `Hade (Jiu-Jitsu)`, `Torite`, and `Hojo-Jitsu`.
- It explicitly describes a staged transmission process including `Omote geiko` and `Ura geiko`.

Important limitation:
- The English pages confirm structured traditional transmission, but do not publish a complete kata list comparable to Kodokan's public pages.

## Practical conclusion

If you need the most trustworthy English online references for jujutsu kata, use:

1. `Koshiki-no-Kata` for preserved classical `Kitō-ryū` forms.
2. `Kime-no-Kata` for formal serious-combat paired kata.
3. `Kodokan Goshin-jutsu` for formal self-defense paired kata.
4. `Takenouchi-ryu official pages` to confirm that traditional jujutsu schools preserve larger kata-based transmission systems beyond these published Kodokan examples.

## Important caveat

These sources support a careful statement:
- there are other traditional and formal kata in jujutsu besides `Koshiki-no-Kata`,
- but official English publication is uneven,
- and Kodokan remains the clearest public English source for named jujutsu-related kata sequences.
