# Kitō-ryū Jujutsu Kata: Reference Sources

Checked on 2026-05-17.

This note uses `jujutsu` for historical accuracy, though `jujitsu` is a common English spelling.

## Reliability note

I did not find a single official English website that exposes the full historical Kitō-ryū kata curriculum as a complete modern public list.

What I did find is stronger in a different way:
- Kodokan is an official and highly authoritative institution directly connected to Kano Jigoro.
- Kodokan explicitly states that `Koshiki-no-Kata` preserves the forms of Kitō-ryū jujutsu with little change.
- Kodokan provides an official English page and an official English textbook PDF listing all 21 preserved techniques.

For English online material, this is the most reliable and defensible source set I found.

## Best sources

### 1. Kodokan Global: Koshiki-no-Kata

Source: <https://kdkjd.org/koshiki-no-kata/>

Why it matters:
- Official Kodokan English page.
- Explicit public name list for the preserved Kitō-ryū-derived form.
- Best quick online reference for the technique names.

Coverage:
- `Omote`: Tai, Yume-no-uchi, Ryoku-hi, Mizu-guruma, Mizu-nagare, Hiki-otoshi, Ko-daore, Uchi-kudaki, Tani-otoshi, Kuruma-daore, Shikoro-dori, Shikoro-gaeshi, Yu-dachi, Taki-otoshi
- `Ura`: Mi-kudaki, Kuruma-gaeshi, Mizu-iri, Ryu-setsu, Saka-otoshi, Yuki-ore, Iwa-nami

### 2. Kodokan KATA Textbook: Koshiki-no-Kata

Source: <https://kdkjd.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/Koshiki-no-Kata.pdf>

Why it matters:
- Official Kodokan English textbook.
- States that Koshiki-no-Kata has its origin in Kitō School of Jujutsu.
- Gives formal opening, names of techniques, and full execution details.

Most important points from the introduction:
- Koshiki-no-Kata originates in Kitō School of Jujutsu.
- Kano retained it almost unchanged as Kodokan `Koshiki-no-Kata`.
- It consists of `14 Omote` and `7 Ura` techniques.
- It preserves armored-combat logic and emphasizes both technical and mental training.

### 3. Kodokan Global: Kata

Source: <https://kdkjd.org/kata/>

Why it matters:
- Official Kodokan overview page for kata.
- Provides the clearest short institutional statement connecting Kodokan `Koshiki-no-Kata` to Kitō-ryū.

Best use:
- High-level statement of legitimacy and preservation lineage.

Key point:
- Kodokan says Kano highly evaluated the forms of Kitō-ryū jujutsu and left them as `Kodokan Koshiki-no-Kata` with little changes.

### 4. Kodokan Global: History of Kodokan Judo

Source: <https://kdkjd.org/%E8%AC%9B%E9%81%93%E9%A4%A8%E6%9F%94%E9%81%93%E3%81%AE%E6%AD%B4%E5%8F%B2/>

Why it matters:
- Official institutional history page.
- Confirms that Kano studied and mastered the teachings of Kitō-ryū before founding Kodokan Judo.

Best use:
- Historical context for why Kodokan material is the strongest official English preservation source for Kitō-ryū kata.

## Most reliable current English reference set

If you need the most trusted English online reference stack for Kitō-ryū kata, use:

1. `Kodokan Koshiki-no-Kata page` for the compact official list.
2. `Kodokan Koshiki-no-Kata textbook PDF` for formal details and execution.
3. `Kodokan Kata overview` for the institutional statement of preservation.
4. `Kodokan history page` for lineage context.

## Important caveat

This reference set is best understood as:
- the most reliable official English online documentation of the `preserved Kitō-ryū forms`,
- not proof that the entire historical Kitō-ryū curriculum is fully exposed online in English by an official institution.

For this repository, that limitation should be stated explicitly rather than hidden.
