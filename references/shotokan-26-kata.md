# Shotokan 26 Kata: English Reference Sources

Checked on 2026-05-17.

This file collects the most reliable English online references I found for the 26 standard Shotokan kata. Priority was given to official organizations and federation-grade documents over blogs, club notes, or SEO pages.

## Best sources

### 1. JKA official kata books

Source: <https://www.jka.or.jp/en/books-and-videos/>

Why it matters:
- JKA explicitly describes these as the authoritative English-language kata books for all JKA-affiliated countries.
- This is the strongest source here for technical study order and official naming.

Coverage:
- Volume 1: Heian Shodan, Heian Nidan, Heian Sandan, Heian Yondan, Heian Godan, Tekki Shodan, Bassai Dai, Kanku Dai, Jion, Enpi
- Volume 2: Gankaku, Jitte, Hangetsu, Tekki Nidan, Tekki Sandan
- Volume 3: Bassai Sho, Kanku Sho, Sochin, Nijyushiho, Unsu
- Volume 4: Wankan, Gojyushiho Dai, Gojyushiho Sho, Meikyo, Chinte

Note:
- These four JKA volumes currently expose 25 kata on the official English page. `Ji'in` is not listed there.

### 2. JKA technical manual for instructors

Source: <https://www.jka.or.jp/wp/wp-content/uploads/2017/04/tech_manual_instructor.pdf>

Why it matters:
- Official JKA manual.
- Useful free reference for move counts and short characteristic notes.

Coverage visible in the manual:
- Heian Shodan, Heian Nidan, Heian Sandan, Heian Yondan, Heian Godan
- Tekki Shodan, Tekki Nidan, Tekki Sandan
- Bassai Dai, Bassai Sho
- Kanku Dai, Kanku Sho
- Jitte, Hangetsu, Enpi, Gankaku, Jion
- Sochin, Nijushiho, Unsu
- Wankan, Gojushiho Dai, Gojushiho Sho, Meikyo, Chinte

Note:
- This manual also gives 25 kata, not 26.

### 3. JKA dan ranking / grading expectations

Source: <https://www.jka.or.jp/en/about-jka/dan-ranking/>

Why it matters:
- Official JKA grading reference.
- Good for understanding which kata JKA treats as core in formal progression and examination.

Useful examples from the page:
- Senior dan exams reference the set from `Heian Shodan` through `Tekki Sandan`.
- Higher dan exams explicitly mention `Bassai Dai`, `Kanku Dai`, `Enpi`, and `Jion`.

### 4. SKIF official FAQ

Source: <https://www.skifworld.com/FAQ.php>

Why it matters:
- SKIF is one of the largest international Shotokan organizations.
- Its FAQ explicitly states that the Shotokan system has 26 kata.

Best use:
- Confirmation that the traditional Shotokan system is treated as a 26-kata system by a major Shotokan federation.

### 5. WUKF official kata list

Source: <https://www.wukf-karate.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/OFFICIAL-WUKF-KATA-LIST-07-APR-2024-20240505.pdf>

Why it matters:
- Official federation PDF.
- Useful as a complete cross-check for the Shotokan kata names, including `Ji'in`.

Best use:
- Supplemental name list when a single free JKA English page does not expose the full 26 at once.

### 6. JKA official YouTube channel entry point

Source page: <https://www.jka.or.jp/en/jka-news/funakoshi-gichin-cup-16th-karate-world-championship-tournament-live-streaming/>

Why it matters:
- JKA links directly from its official site to the official JKA YouTube channel.
- Best official video starting point when you want to compare timing, rhythm, and tournament-level execution.

Channel:
- <https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCmA1UdAKQHseE76uYikYVuA>

## Recommended way to use these references

If you want the most trustworthy study stack, use them in this order:

1. `JKA kata books` for the main technical reference.
2. `JKA technical manual` for move counts and concise characteristics.
3. `JKA dan ranking` for progression and exam relevance.
4. `SKIF FAQ` plus `WUKF official kata list` to validate the full 26-kata frame and cover `Ji'in`.
5. `JKA official YouTube channel` for demonstration footage.

## Important caveat

I did not find a single free English official JKA web page that fully documents all 26 kata together.

What the official material supports cleanly:
- JKA provides the strongest technical references for 25 kata.
- SKIF explicitly states Shotokan has 26 kata.
- WUKF provides an official list that includes `Ji'in`.

For a Cyberjutsu reference set, this is the most defensible combination I found without falling back to lower-trust commercial or hobbyist pages.
